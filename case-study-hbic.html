Head Bartender in Charge

We turned HBIC's curriculum into a branded, platform-native series—shot for vertical and horizontal, packaged with graphics, and ready to post anywhere.

Our Process

From Curriculum to Content Series
A systematic approach to educational content

01

Identify Problems

Strong brand, no repeatable content system.

HBIC had recipes, voice, and community, but not a scalable series format. Videos needed to feel native to Reels/YouTube/TikTok, keep a consistent look, and support the subscription model—without rebuilding from scratch each episode.

02

Discover Opportunities

A class-to-camera kit built for batching.

We defined an episode blueprint—hook → ingredients → technique → pour/garnish → quick CTA—plus shot lists, short scripts, and a creator-style set. Every recipe is captured in 9:16 and 16:9, with on-screen ingredient cards, captions for silent autoplay, and recurring themes (Home Bar 101, Spirit Deep Dives, Classics Remixed).

03

Refine the Creative Vision

A branded tutorial system that ships everywhere.

Deliverables include master templates (9:16/16:9/1:1), lower thirds, ingredient graphics, motion open/close, music beds, thumbnails, and SEO copy blocks. The library is tagged by base spirit, technique, and difficulty, with a style guide so new episodes slot in cleanly—and promos can point to HBIC modules, virtual happy hours, and live events.
