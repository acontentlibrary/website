Hunter Douglas.

We turned client shot lists into polished, vertical content, captured on iPhone, aligned to platform trends, and ready to post the same day.

Our Process

From Shot Lists to Social Ready
A systematic approach to mobile-first content

01

Identify Problems

Planned ideas, but not platform-ready or scalable.

Hunter Douglas had concepts and locations, but needed authentic, mobile-first video that matched Reels/TikTok norms, looked natural (not "ad-y"), and could be produced quickly across campaigns. Existing workflows were slow, horizontal-biased, and inconsistent in framing, captions, and file delivery.

02

Discover Opportunities

Lean, native capture + repeatable social formats.

We shifted to iPhone-native, vertical masters with creator-style framing, quick hooks, and trend-aware pacing. Batch shoots at each location yielded multiple formats per scene (how-to, before/after, swipe-through, testimonial), plus baked-in captions for silent autoplay and easy cross-posting.

03

Refine the Creative Vision

A simple playbook that ships fast and stays on brand.

We defined a light shot ladder (wide context → feature close-ups → payoff), text/overlay rules, logo safe-areas, and color/lighting guidelines. Deliverables included 6–15s and 30s cutdowns, thumbnails, SRT captions, and organized folders with clear naming—so teams can grab, post, and reuse without re-editing.
